Hurricanes

Large rotating storms with high speed winds that form over warm waters in tropical, warm ocean water areas and move toward land

MORE ABOUT HURRICANES Home

CAUSES OF HURRICANES

Hurricane Sandy

Date: From Oct 22, 2012 to Nov 2, 2012
Damage: $68.7 billion (2012 USD); (Fifth-costliest hurricane in U.S. history)

They start when warm, moist air from the ocean surface begins to rise rapidly, where it encounters cooler air that causes the warm water vapor to condense and to form storm clouds and drops of rain.

They also form because of differences between zones of high and low pressure, colliding to produce pressure.

CHARACTERISTICS OF HURRICANES

Hurricane Katrina
Date: From August 23, 2005 to August 31, 2005
Damage: $125 billion (2005 USD); (Tied as costliest tropical cyclone on record)

They have sustained winds of at least 74 miles per hour and an area of low air pressure in the center called the eye.

Coriolis effect: Helps make the spins in hurricanes; is the phenomena where the rotation of a free-moving object such as wind is influenced by the spinning of the Earth -- Wind in the Northern Hemisphere deflects to the right. Wind in the Southern Hemisphere moves to the left.

SCIENTIFIC NAME

Tropical Cyclone

WHY ARE HURRICANES A THREAT?

  • They are considered one of nature's most powerful storms.

  • They include powerful winds, heavy rainfall, storm surges, coastal and inland flooding, rip currents, tornadoes, and landslides.

  • They are capable of causing substantial destruction.

  • Storm Surge is an abnormal rise of water generated by a storm's winds. Storm surge can travel several miles inland.

  • In estuaries and bayous, salt water intrusion endangers public health and the environment.

WHERE IS IT THE HIGHEST RISK?

The Atlantic Coast, the Gulf of Mexico, and the Hawaiian Islands
